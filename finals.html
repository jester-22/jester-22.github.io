Why HAP-PWD?

HAP-PWD stands as a pillar in the realm of digital education, dedicated to providing vital resources and fostering a supportive community that is instrumental for the educational advancement and professional development of persons with disabilities (PWDs). Our platform is meticulously crafted to bridge the gap in education accessibility, offering an inclusive environment where learning knows no barriers.

Comprehensive Approach to Learning

At HAP-PWD, we believe in a holistic approach to education. Our platform is not just a repository of information but a vibrant ecosystem teeming with opportunities for growth, collaboration, and empowerment. From interactive courses and workshops to forums for sharing experiences and insights, we provide a multifaceted experience that caters to the unique needs and aspirations of our learners.

Empowering PWDs for a Brighter Future

Our mission extends beyond education; we are committed to equipping PWDs with the skills and confidence needed to thrive in the professional world. By offering job readiness programs, career guidance, and connections to employment opportunities, we strive to dismantle barriers and create a more inclusive society where PWDs are recognized for their talents and potential.

PWD'S RIGHT TO EDUCATION

Below is part of RA 7277 and its Implementing Rules and Regulations on Education for Persons with Disabilities. The article is uploaded to educate the public on PWDs' right to education.
Any problems relating to or in violation of RA 7277 can be coursed to the National Council on Disability Affairs at 9516033 or Department of Education at 632-1361.
RA 7277 CHAPTER 2 Section 12 to 14

Access to Quality Education

The State shall ensure that disabled persons are provided with adequate access to quality education and ample opportunities to develop their skills. It shall take appropriate steps to make such education accessible to all disabled persons. It shall be unlawful for any learning institutions to deny a disabled person admission to any course it offers by reason of handicap or disability. The State shall take into consideration the special requirements of disabled persons in the formulation of education policies and program. It shall encourage learning institutions to take into account the special needs of disabled persons with respect to the use of school facilities, class schedules, physical education requirements and other pertinent consideration. The State shall also promote the provision by learning institutions, of auxiliary services that will facilitate the learning process for disabled persons.

Special Education

The State shall establish, maintain and support a complete, adequate and integrated system of special education for the visually impaired, hearing impaired, mentally retarded persons and other type of exceptional children in all regions of the country. Towards this end, the Department of Education, Culture and Sports shall establish special education classes in public schools in cities, or municipalities. It shall also establish, where viable, Braille and Record Libraries in provinces, cities or municipalities. The National Government shall allocate funds necessary for the effective implementation of the special education program nationwide. Local government units may likewise appropriate counterpart funds to supplement national funds. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR RA7277)

Assistive Technology Resources

Assistive technology refers to a wide range of products, equipment, and systems designed to support people with disabilities. These tools aim to enhance learning, working, and daily living. They can be physical devices (like wheelchairs, hearing aids, or prosthetic limbs) or digital solutions (such as speech recognition software or time management apps).



Assistive technology helps maintain or improve an individual's functioning related to cognition, communication, hearing, mobility, self-care, and vision. By doing so, it enables health, well-being, inclusion, and participation

  • Text-to-Speech (TTS) Assistive Tools

    Software or devices that convert written text into spoken words, allowing individuals with visual impairments, learning disabilities, or other challenges to access digital content more easily

    • Speechify
      •
    • Synthesys
    • Murf
    • Descript
    • Speechelo

  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)

    Refers to the methods and devices designed to help people with speech or language problems to communicate. These can include simple systems like pictures, gestures, and pointing, as well as more complex techniques involving powerful computer technology.

    • Fluent AAC
    • Proloquo2Go
    • Speak for Yourself
    • Avaz AAC
    • TD Snap AAC
    • Sono Flex
    • TouchChat HD - AAC

  • Speech-to-Text (STT) Software

    This type of software converts spoken words into written text, allowing individuals with mobility impairments or speech impairments to communicate more easily.

    • Google Voice Typing: An embedded tool in Google Docs that allows users to convert spoken words into typed text.
    • Microsoft Speech Recognition: A built-in feature in Windows 10 that allows you to control the entire Windows OS with speech.
    • Microsoft 365 Voice Dictation: A speech-to-text capability built directly into Microsoft 365 products such as Word, PowerPoint, Outlook, and OneNote.
    • Mac OS Voice Control: A feature in System Preferences that allows Mac users to dictate text and control the operating system using their voice.
    • Dragon Naturally Speaking: A voice recognition software designed to transcribe spoken words into digital text, compatible with Microsoft Office.

  • Screen readers

    These are software programs that allow blind or visually impaired individuals to use a computer. They work by reading aloud the text that appears on the screen, including menus, dialog boxes, and web pages.

    • NVDA (NonVisual Desktop Access)
    • Serotek System Access
    • Apple VoiceOver
    • ORCA (Open Source Computer Accessibility)
    • BRLTTY (Braille Display Teletypewriter Driver)
    • Emacspeak
    • WebAnywhere
    • ChromeVox
    • ChromeVis
    • Narrator (Microsoft Windows)

  • Screen magnification software

    Allows users to control the size of text and/or graphics on the screen.

    • ZoomText
    • MAGic
    • SuperNova
    • iZoom

  • Braille Smartwatch

    Allows users to receive and read messages in Braille, providing independence for the visually impaired.

    • DOT Watch App
    • BrailleBack
    • BRLTTY
    • VoiceOver

  • Cognitive Aids

    Assist individuals with cognitive impairments in tasks like remembering information or organizing thoughts.

    • Rehametrics
    • Cueing/Memory Aid
    • Educational Software
    • Speech Recognition
    • Thinking Time Pro

  • Talking Calculators

    Aid individuals with visual impairments or learning disabilities in performing mathematical calculations.

    • Talking Calculator by RWF Talking Software
    • My Talking Calculator
    • CalculatorLibrary
    • My Talking Calculator (Google Play)

  • Mobility Aids

    Devices or equipment that help individuals with mobility impairments to move around more easily and safely.

    • Wheelchairs (These devices provide mobility for people with physical impairments. They come in various types, including manual and electric wheelchairs)
    • Walkers (Assist individuals with stability and balance while walking.)
    • Scooters (Electric mobility devices designed for outdoor use.)
    • Canes (Support walking and help maintain balance.)
    • Crutches (Aid in walking and weight-bearing.)

  • Hearing Aids

    These devices amplify sound for individuals with hearing loss or impairment.

    • Completely in the canal (CIC) or mini CIC (Smallest, fit inside ear canal, suitable for mild to moderate hearing loss.)
    • In the canal (ITC) (Fit partially in ear canal, suitable for mild to moderate hearing loss.)
    • In the ear (ITE) ( Fit in outer ear, available in full shell and half shell, suitable for mild to severe hearing loss.)
    • Behind the ear (BTE) (Hook over ear, suitable for all ages and types of hearing loss.)
    • Receiver in canal or receiver in the ear (Similar to BTE but with a speaker in the ear canal, suitable for mild to severe hearing loss.)
    • Open fit (BTE with a thin tube or receiver-in-canal, suitable for better low-frequency hearing and mild to moderate high-frequency hearing loss.)
